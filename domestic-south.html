Domestic Page

South &sightseeing

1. Nashville, Tennesse
For all things country, Nashville is a must for music lovers. Nashville was home to artists like Taylor Swift, Billy Ray Cyrus, and Dolly Patron, whose influences lie within the city's culture. The Country Music Hall of Fame features the history of the country and exhibits surrounding the genre's award-winning singers. A trip to Nashville is excellent for those looking for great music.

2. New Orleans, Louisiana
New Orleans, otherwise known as the "Big Easy," is one of the most vibrant cities in the US. The city offers a vibrant nightlife filled with music and cuisine. Catch Mardis Gras in New Orleans, or listen to the famous sounds of jazz, R&B, and soul. Food like gumbo and jambalaya are must-haves. A good time is guaranteed in New Orleans.

3. Savannah, Georgia
Savannah, Georgia is home to some of the most beautiful landscapes in the South. State parks like Skidaway Island State Park feature native marshes, forests, and salt flats. Cumberland Island offers beaches, campsites, and trails to discover. And, don't forget the Wildlife Refugees, like Harris Neck National Wildlife Refuge. Savannah provides a look at remarkable native Southern ecosystems and overall stunning nature.

4. Charleston, NC
Charleston, North Carolina is marked with great American history surrounding the Civil War. Its architecture hints at the past, with cobblestone streets and antebellum homes. Fort Sumter, where the war starts, is available to visit across the water. Charleston is the place to tour for a take on the history of the Civil War.
